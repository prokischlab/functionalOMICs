Overview of variants used for OddsPath calculation

Dmitrii Smirnov

2021-07-09

Variant summary

For calculation of the odds of pathogenicity (OddsPath), data from 130 genetically diagnosed individuals from Yepez et al. 2021 study was used. Due to data privacy policy, genetic data, available in-house, was not shared.

Pathogenic variants

Variants, reported as disease-causing (pathogenic) were considered

# vtp <- fread("data/pathogenic_variants.tsv")

Benign variants

Variants, detected in genetically diagnosed individuals, were considered benign if following criteria were met: 1) Rare variants (MAF < 0.01 in gnomAD) reported “Benign” or “Likely benign” in ClinVar and were classified as “Benign” with InterVar 2) Frequent (MAF > 0.05 in gnomAD) stop and frameshift variants not reported in ClinVar
